1 · The Gemba — home / Epic Kanban

The factory-floor view. Six columns, cards-are-Epics, drag-to-stage as primary action.

The Gemba is the home screen: a six-column Epic Kanban (Backlog → Next Up → Staged → In Progress → Done → Canceled) rendered with the Marker project's 8 Epics. Sidebar is collapsed by default on the Board to maximise the Gemba view (§4.1). The yellow active-walk banner across the top announces the in-progress Gemba walk without occupying chrome permanently.

Primary interaction rendered: the operator is mid-drag on mk-e4 (browser extension), pulling it from Next Up toward a dashed ghost outline in Staged. A card ghost follows the cursor. Per §4.5 the drop is nonce-gated in supervised mode; the ghost copy reads "Drop to Stage · nonce-gated · supervised mode" so the mutation surface is honest about its confirmation path.

Epic cards (§4.2) show priority stripe, title, readiness counts, token-budget gauge (on mk-e2 at 62%, amber), parallel-group glyph (A on mk-e2 and mk-e3), escalation dot on mk-e6, and the purview-violation yellow-outlined dot on mk-e8. The PM panel at the bottom is in its default context-sensitive state, with quick-action buttons scoped to Board ("Recommend order", "What remains", "Trim to budget", "Dispatch readiness"). Advisor cost is displayed separately from agent cost in the top-bar budget gauge per the `gm-e11.2` separation.

Right-side overlay on dimmed Board. Mini-kanban with stage swimlanes.

4 · Plan view — staging surface

Pick what runs next and in what parallel group. Drag-between-clusters scope toggle.

Plan (§5.1) is the batch-launch surface. Sidebar is expanded (it's not the Board, §2.2). The Execute all Staged primary button carries a "2 epics · ~$2.40" sub-badge; the cost-preview row below quantifies spend (~142k tokens · ~3h wall-clock · share-of-sprint-budget stacked bar).

Staged work is clustered by parallel group: Group A (blue outline, parallel safety verified) contains mk-e3 (CLI, Staged) and mk-e2 (Web UI, already running — rendered with an inset "running now" blue pill and live-spend readout). Group B (tan dashed outline, "eligible to merge into A") contains mk-e5. Below those, a red-X cluster marks Not parallel-safe with A — mk-e4 (Browser extension) is gated because its file signature overlaps cmd/marker/* with mk-e3.

Primary interaction rendered: the operator drags mk-e5 by its handle out of Group B toward Group A. A ghost row follows the cursor with a Join Group A affirmation chip, and the Group B source row is dimmed with "dragging to group A…" italic annotation. Bottom-of-view "Next candidates" previews mk-e7 (Backlog, not-yet-stageable) with a "Stage when ready — needs DoD review" guidance per the "Every Epic requires DoD before Start" guardrail.

5 · Persona roster — Coaches & Managers

11 configured personas. PM card expanded showing PPPP + skills + budget.

The roster (§5.5) renders as a grid of 4-wide persona cards across 3 rows (plus one empty placeholder for the 12th slot and a "Browse Packs" anchor). Each closed-state card shows the icon, role name, variety badge (purple for Coach, navy for Manager — per §1.2's reserved semantics and §3.4 Ask/Consult distinction), the enabled toggle, and the currently-configured model tucked into the metadata line.

Primary interaction rendered: the Project Manager card is selected (navy-outlined, caret pointing down) and its expanded drawer is pinned below the grid with a connector arrow. The drawer surfaces the four PPPP axes ratified by gm-9rv / gm-uf7: Personality ("Precise and economical..."), Perspective ("Coordination, dep-graph awareness..."), Purview explicitly rendered as "(none — PM is orchestrator, not domain-owner)" with an INVARIANT reminder — PM never gates transitions. A Prompt Preview block ships the top-layer value injections (§3.8).

The right column lists the 12 opt-in Skills (with an "+ add skill" affordance), the Budget Policy ($0.25/invocation · $20/day caps, 3.7% cap-pressure), and Context Providers (5 of 7 opted in — code_summary and call_graph available but off, per persona-level opt-in). The operator's cursor is hovering the navy Edit button, which per §5.5 opens a modal TOML editor over .gemba/personas/pm.toml (tooltip confirms the target path). The footer aggregates: 11 personas · 6 Coaches · 5 Managers · $1.51 advisor spend today · CTO pack active.
